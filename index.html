Cuora couro couro (Schweigger, 1812)

Local status: Near Threatened

Global status: Endangered

Specific ephibet derives from Malay word for turtle "kura-kura".

Description:

Medium sized geoemydid. Balck to brownish carapace and weakly veterbral keel (sometimes expressed as a cream coloured strip in older individuals).
Hinged plastron with variable sized dark blotches on each plastral scute while males sometimes have a dark pigementation at the medial plastral area.
Absent of anal notch. Plastral fromula for male:An>Ab>G>F>H and female: Ab>An>P>G>F>H.

Distribution & Ecology:

Wide-spread but uncommon locally. Inhabits streams, swamps and ponds in both forests and parklands. Omnivorous.

Global distribution: Borneo, Indonesia (Sumatra, Java & Bali), Peninsular Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand and the Philippines
